Welcome to Wayanad, the Land of Nature.

Explore the lush landscapes, rich history, and vibrant culture of Wayanad, Kerala's hidden gem. From breathtaking views to age-old traditions, there's something for everyone.

Discover Wayanad

Landmarks

Famous Historical Landmarks

Wayanad is home to several ancient and historical landmarks. Discover the stories behind these iconic places.

Edakkal Caves

These caves contain ancient carvings dating back to the Stone Age. A must-visit for history lovers.

Banasura Sagar Dam

The largest earthen dam in India, offering beautiful views of the surrounding hills and waters.

Karapuzha Dam

An impressive dam with scenic surroundings, ideal for picnics and nature walks.

Culture

Explore the Cultural Heritage of Wayanad

From traditional festivals to folk dances, Wayanad has a rich cultural scene.

Festivals

Celebrate vibrant festivals like Onam and Vishu that bring the community together.

Traditional Dance

Enjoy traditional dance forms like Oppana and Theyyam, performed during special occasions.

Music

Listen to the soulful music of Kerala that adds rhythm to every festival and celebration.

Cuisine

Taste the Local Flavors of Wayanad

Wayanad's cuisine is a fusion of traditional Kerala recipes with unique ingredients found in the region.

Appam and Stew

A popular breakfast dish, soft appams served with a rich coconut stew.

Malabar Biryani

A flavorful biryani from the Malabar region, filled with spices and tender meat.

Payasam

A sweet delicacy made with milk, rice, and jaggery, served during festivals.
